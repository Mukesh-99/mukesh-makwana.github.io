About Me

I am a cognitive scientist and researcher currently affiliated with the Donoghue lab at Brown University, where I investigate neural mechanisms of reaching and grasping actions, and explore sense-of-agency in individuals using brain-computer interfaces. With a Ph.D. in Cognitive Science from the University of Allahabad, India and extensive postdoctoral research experience at Brown University, I bring a unique perspective to UX research that combines deep understanding of human cognition with practical application.

My research spans perception, attention, time perception, and human action‚Äîall fundamental to understanding user experience. I have published in top-tier journals including PLOS Computational Biology, iScience, JEP:HPP, AP&P, Scientific Reports, and Mindfulness, and have presented at major conferences in cognitive science and vision science. My expertise in experimental design, quantitative analysis, and human behavioral research translates directly to UX research methodologies.

I am passionate about applying scientific rigor and cognitive insights to improve educational technology and learning experiences. My experience with experimental design, data analysis, and understanding human cognition positions me to conduct user research that is both methodologically sound and practically impactful.

Cognitive Psychology | Sports Psychology
